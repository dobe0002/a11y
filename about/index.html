Why accessibility?

Over the years, I've found that many folks become involved in the web accessibility community for a lot of different reasons. For many it is stemming from personal experiences. For others, it might be from an advocacy perspective. Others are involved motivated by legal impacts. I feel that all of these (and others) are valid, but they happen to be different than my motivations.

I have always been a strong User Centered Design advocate. The core of UCD is the end users and all the glorious flavors they come in. I found it very problematic that many UCD folks talked about users with disabilities and/or users of assistive technologies as separate than the "normal" end users. These folks ARE part of our end users and should not be treated as separate. At heart, I dont' believe accessibility should be a separate "thing" beyond normal development, design, or UX - but I've found that many folks aren't ready for that yet, so I (begrudgingly) continue to use the term accessibility.

I've also been an advocate of standards-driven development while creating code that is easy to maintain. Professionals use standards. Professionals write easy-to-maintain code. Accessible code is both standards based, and usually easier to maintain. Again, accessibility shouldn't be a separate skill, but something that, we as professionals, just do. Period.

I realise that we all aren't there yet and that is OK as long as we move forward. That is why I am here spending many late nights trying to help folks grow. I know there are many folks in the accessibility community that would shame folks for not knowing enough about accessibility or try to "one up" others with their accessibility skills. I, on the other hand, am exited when folks want to learn more. No shame, no "one upping", not threatened by other's abilities.

My definition of accessibility

There are many definitions of what it means when a web site or web application is accessible. I do like the POUR principles, but have tweaked it a little bit to form my own definition:

Web accessibility means that all users regardless of technology can:
  • perceive
  • understand
  • navigate
  • interact
  • and contribute to
the web.

I have heard numerous definitions over the years. Many of them, I would consider, incomplete. This isn't to say that folks who have said them are uninformed or of less intellegence. Instead, I think folks are just trying to wrap their heads this whole accessibility thing and have just started on their own journy.
